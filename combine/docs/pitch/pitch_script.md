# Pitch video script (~3 min)

Recorded on the **live, deployed** product, not a local dev build (§33), so what judges see matches what ships.

## Beat sheet

**0:00 – 0:20 — The hook.** A familiar WhatsApp forward on screen. VO: "You've seen a message like this. You're not sure. So you forward it — just in case." Beat. "By the time a fact-check appears, it's already in a hundred groups."

**0:20 – 0:40 — The turn.** "Everyone fights fake news in the present. ORACLE goes back in time." Show the one-line pitch. Wordmark.

**0:40 – 1:40 — The demo (golden path, one continuous take).**
- Paste the message. One tap: *Scan for truth*.
- Scanning checklist ticks through.
- **Origin:** "Born on Facebook — Belgium, January 2020." Tag pills.
- **Mutation:** markers appear country by country — spread across N countries. Show the wording drift card.
- **Damage:** the 2×2 counters animate up from zero. **On this beat, duck the audio to near-silence** (§7.6) — this is the emotional low point. Hold on the real number and its source.

**1:40 – 2:10 — The payoff.** The **truth card**: capture-flash, verdict badge, "You broke the chain. It ends with you." Tap *Save & share* → the OS share sheet → back into the group. Then the **Legacy Wall**: the new catch bounces in.

**2:10 – 2:40 — Why it's trustworthy.** "Every number is sourced. When we don't know, we say so." Cut to the web dashboard: live "chains broken" counter, spread map, leaderboard.

**2:40 – 3:00 — Close + the ask.** The real pilot number: "In our pilot, people who saw the origin story were **[X]** less likely to re-share." Logo. Prototype URL. "Play your part."

## Notes
- The audio-duck on the damage counters must feel identical to the live product (the app fires a volume-ducking callback at counter start).
- Keep copy sentence case; no exclamation points on screen (§8.7).
- Replace **[X]** with the real pilot ratio before recording (§34).
