# Pilot test protocol (§16.7)

The pitch makes a specific behavioural claim — *seeing the origin story makes people less likely to re-share.* This must be backed by a **real, documented pilot**, not an automated test or an invented number.

## Hypothesis
Participants who see the full ORACLE result (origin + mutation + damage) are less likely to forward a later, similar piece of misinformation than participants who see only a plain "this is false" verdict.

## Design
- **Participants:** 20, recruited with informed consent. Randomly split into Group A (10) and Group B (10).
- **Intervention (day 0):** both groups are shown the *same* piece of misinformation.
  - Group A sees a plain "this is false" verdict.
  - Group B sees the full ORACLE result (origin, mutation timeline, damage report).
- **Measurement (day 7):** send both groups a *similar, new* piece of misinformation via WhatsApp. Within 48 hours, record how many people in each group forward it onward.

## What we record
- Group sizes and the exact two stimuli used.
- Forwarding counts per group within the 48-hour window.
- The real ratio, whatever it is (including a null/negative result), with a one-paragraph methodology.

## Ethics & privacy
- Informed consent; participants may withdraw at any time.
- No sensitive personal data stored; results reported in aggregate only.
- Debrief all participants afterwards, including that a stimulus was known misinformation.

## Timeline
Runs two calendar weeks, so **start as soon as Phase 3 completes** (in parallel with Phases 4–5).

## Output
The measured ratio replaces every placeholder statistic in the product copy, [proposal.md](proposal.md), and the pitch video (§32, §34).
